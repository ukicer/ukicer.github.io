Keynote

Keynote speaker: Professor Alan Blackwell

Title: Learning AI Fundamentals

Abstract: Recent developments in generative AI, as with earlier digital transformations that resulted from personal computing, internet access, smartphones and web search, are again changing public understanding of what kind of tool a computer can be. Policy makers appreciate that there may be a need for “AI literacy,” but definitions of what this might entail are often driven by advocacy from businesses whose primary motivation is to train a new generation of customer for their products. This talk will consider alternative perspectives, including principles such as information theory and Bayesian statistics that may need greater emphasis in curricula, and the challenge of educating critical technical practitioners.

Biography: Alan Blackwell is Professor of Interdisciplinary Design at the University of Cambridge Department of Computer Science and Technology. Following a professional career as an AI Engineer since the 1980s, he has undertaken study in Cognitive Psychology, Comparative Religion, and Music. In addition to research in computational art and design practice, he brings a critical perspective to his work as co-Director of the Interdisciplinary Research Centre Cambridge Global Challenges. His book Moral Codes: Designing Alternatives to AI is published by MIT Press.
