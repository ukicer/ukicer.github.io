Critical Research Review (CRR) Workshop

The CRR workshop is for established researchers in computing education research. The aspiration is to bring together individuals to act as critical friends to discuss and nurture ideas and projects that are in progress, and receive feedback from peers. The 'works' from participants vary in terms of type, from research papers to projects, and in progress, from embryonic to near-completion.

There are still relatively few active researchers in computing education in the UK and Ireland, and even fewer research groups of knowledgeable peers and a supportive environment for developing ideas.

The aim of the CRR workshop is to give, and receive, feedback on computing education research. Each participant has an equal allocation of time to present their work (circulated in advance). Each person presents in turn, and all participants discuss, comment, question, and provide feedback. In this way the CRR gives access to other researchers and their expertise and provides a community context for the work presented.

"… the whole group is learning together at any given moment, not just the presenter. Everyone benefits from the collective thinking about problems"

A CRR workshop is an excellent opportunity to discuss and develop ongoing work. The best submissions are concrete and anchored in a specific topic.

“ … you're able to get feedback about in-progress work. The point is not to share work that you've completed, but rather to bring something that you're working on and not sure about so that you can get formative feedback.”

CRR submissions may be centred on various elements of research, including (but not limited to):

  • A rejected paper
  • A kernel of an idea
  • A grant proposal
  • A research project
  • A study design
  • An analysis approach

If accepted, attendees prepare a short primer on their work (4 page maximum) which will be circulated in advance for everyone to read. The goal of this is to give just enough information to be useful, but not so much that readers don’t know where to focus.

Depending on the number of participants we can expect to spend 30 - 45 minutes discussing each person’s work.

Note: The CRR workshop is for established researchers in computing education research. It is not an appropriate venue for PhD students to present their PhD work: this should be taken to the UKICER Doctoral Consortium.

Applying for the CRR Workshop

To apply to participate in the CRR workshop, please submit a 1- or 2-page overview of the project you'll present to the group. Also, include a brief description of previous research and areas of expertise you are able to offer the group. While no specific format is required, your submission should include:

  • Your affiliation and title
  • Project title and description (1–3 paragraphs)
  • Stage of the project when CRR will occur (conceptual, new, on-going, near complete)
  • Type of feedback sought (ideally, what would you most like from CRR participants? Research questions, design, critique, related works?)
  • Your research experience (qualitative, quantitative, or mixed methods; study design; human subjects research; K-12, undergraduate, graduate, or professional contexts)
  • Submit your application via EasyChair.

Accepted participants must prepare and submit a white paper (2–4 pages) to serve as a primer for all workshop participants. White papers are not published in the conference proceedings.
